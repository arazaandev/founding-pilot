# Discovery call guide

Use this guide for a 30-minute conversation with a technology-focused recruitment agency. The purpose is to understand past behaviour and decide the next step, not to sell a hypothetical platform.

## Before the call

- Review the agency's website, specializations, geography, and visible proof.
- Record the agency in `templates/agency-outreach-tracker.csv`.
- Write down no more than three assumptions to test.
- Ask permission to take notes. Do not record the call without explicit consent.

## 0–3 minutes: frame the conversation

> I am learning how recruitment agencies decide which employers are worth approaching and what evidence makes outreach timely. I would like to focus on real examples. I may show you Lancara's calibration offer near the end, but this is not a product demonstration.

Confirm the participant's role, involvement in winning employer clients, and available time.

## 3–12 minutes: reconstruct the last success

1. Tell me about the last new employer client your agency won.
2. How did that company first enter your pipeline?
3. What made it worth approaching at that moment?
4. What evidence did you have before the first contact?
5. Who researched and approved the account? How long did that take?
6. What happened between selecting the account and winning the work?

Prompts: role family, vacancy pattern, company event, relationship, internal capacity, stakeholder, time spent. Ask “What happened next?” before asking for an opinion.

## 12–20 minutes: selection and rejection

1. Describe a company that looked attractive but was rejected or deprioritized.
2. What disqualified it?
3. Which employer, role, geography, fee, or delivery conditions make work commercially attractive?
4. Which hiring conditions create genuine urgency rather than ordinary vacancy volume?
5. What must be verified before a salesperson contacts an account?
6. How do existing clients, active prospects, or candidate strengths change the decision?

Capture exact phrases in `objection-and-language-log.md`. Do not translate them into Lancara's taxonomy during the call.

## 20–26 minutes: test the calibration

Briefly explain: Lancara configures a Hiring Demand Playbook, manually researches three opportunities, shows evidence and uncertainty, and reviews the results with the agency within five business days after complete inputs.

1. What would need to be in a brief for your team to use it?
2. Who would review it, and what would they do if they approved it?
3. What would prevent your team from acting on an apparently relevant brief?
4. If the calibration proved useful, how would a Rp4 million four-week pilot be evaluated and approved?
5. Who besides you would need to agree?

Ask for a reaction, not reassurance. Do not negotiate a new price during discovery.

## 26–30 minutes: commitment and close

Agree one concrete next step: share ICP information, introduce the decision-maker, review a sample, schedule calibration, or close the conversation. Confirm the owner and date.

Immediately after the call:

- Complete the qualification checklist.
- Update the outreach tracker.
- Add verbatim language and objections.
- Mark facts, interpretations, and hypotheses separately.
- Record what changed, what was contradicted, and what must remain unchanged.

## Interviewer rules

- Prefer a recent example over “would you” questions.
- Ask one question at a time; tolerate silence.
- Do not treat enthusiasm as commitment.
- Do not imply that hiring signals prove buying intent.
- Do not promise automation, data sources, or outcomes outside the current offer.
- Stop collecting information that is not needed for recruitment-agency qualification or calibration.
