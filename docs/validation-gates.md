# Validation gates and decision rules

Advance on observed commitments, not compliments. Review evidence at each gate and record the decision, owner, and date.

| Gate | Minimum evidence | What it permits |
|---|---|---|
| A — Messaging discovery | Three qualified agency conversations | Refine language, onboarding, and tentative taxonomy; no major product build |
| B — Output validation | Three agencies review real briefs and decisions are recorded | Improve research method, score, brief structure, and minimal feedback storage |
| C — Payment validation | At least one agency pays Rp4 million upfront | Build only recurring delivery features supported by usage |
| D — Repeatability | A second paid agency, renewal, paid continuation, or qualified referral | Test repeatable acquisition and delivery |

## Continue the recruitment focus when

- Agencies understand the offer without extensive explanation.
- Later calibration briefs are often approved and approved accounts are actually used.
- At least one qualified agency pays.
- Delivery time and cost leave a plausible path to sustainability.

## Revise the offer when

- Agencies value the research but will not pay Rp4 million.
- Usefulness is limited to a much narrower specialization.
- Agencies value monitoring but not stakeholder or outreach guidance.
- The primary value proves to be account research rather than opportunity identification.

## Reconsider the vertical when

- Agencies already solve account sourcing adequately.
- Hiring signals rarely justify outreach.
- Warm introductions are required for nearly every employer.
- Free briefs are praised repeatedly but not used.
- Multiple qualified agencies refuse meaningful commitments of money, time, or reputation.
- Delivery effort makes Rp4 million economically unrealistic.

Do not apply these rules to a single anecdote. Record contrary evidence and sample limitations whenever a decision is made.
